Pricing Related

How is the total fare/price calculated for an Outstation / Multi-city return trip (Inter-city) cab service?

Intercity car fares constitute of various components as below:

  1. Per KM Rate: This is base rate which will be charged on every km travelled. The rate varies with car model and city.
  2. Minimum KM charged per day: We charge minimum distance of 250 kms per day for each city. Example for clarity: If you are using the vehicle for 4 days the minimum we will charge you is 4*250=1000 km, in case your actual usage for the three days (combined) is less (say 800 km) we will still charge you for 1000 km and in case your usage is higher (say 1150), we will charge you for your actual km used.
  3. Daily Driver allowances: For intercity travel customers, you need to pay driver allowance. The allowance amount might vary cab or city wise but ranges from Rs 200 to 500. Apart from it night allowance also charged if driver runs the vehicle during night hours (10pm to 5am).
  4. Toll, parking and state taxes: These will be charged on actual basis during/at end of the journey. You may askdriverto provide the relevant receipts if possible
  5. Definition of a day: Day is defined as calendar day i.e. 12 midnight to next day 12 midnight for all the calculation purposes.
  6. Service Tax: We charge service tax as per Govt rules, its 5.6% of total trip cost now-a-days.

How is the total fare/price calculated for a One Way Drop?

For One Way Drop, we charge fixed cost for the trip as mentioned on our website exclusive of state, toll and service tax.
